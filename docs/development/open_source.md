# Open Source Positioning

RDS is intended as a general research infrastructure project, not a private notebook template for one lab or one field.

## Target Users

- graduate students
- postdocs
- research assistants
- small R&D teams
- labs that want local, durable AI-assisted discussion records

## Early Validation Goals

Early validation should cover multiple research modes:

- wet lab projects with protocols, raw data, figures, and assay interpretation
- computational projects with datasets, pipelines, experiments, and reproducibility needs
- social science projects with research design, data, coding decisions, and limitations
- theoretical projects with problem statements, proof attempts, assumptions, and failed paths
- mixed projects that need handoff between experiments, analysis, and writing

## Generalization Rule

Field-specific affordances should live in examples or scaffold-specific files unless they represent a broader research pattern. Core RDS files should remain field-neutral.

## Distribution Goals

- usable without programming knowledge through AI-tool workflows
- installable as a plugin or skill pack where supported
- inspectable as plain Markdown and small scripts
- forkable and modifiable by labs
- independent of any one AI provider
